Player Stats

Wins above replacement by a position player (WARs_nonpitcher): take offensive, baserunning, and fielding runs created by a pitcher and converts them into total runs created above a replacement player. Converts this into a win total

On Base Percentage (OBPs): total times reaching base divided by total plate appearances

Batting average (AVGs): calculated by taking total number of hits divided by total number of plate appearances

Slugging Percentage (SLGs): derived by dividing the total number of bases gained (e.g. a double is 2 bases) by the total number of PAs; a decent proxy for power hitting ability

Base running ability (UBRs): Measures baserunning ability. An average baserunner has an UBR of zero

Weighted Runs Created (WRCs): Attempts to quantify runs created, except that this stat normalizes the league average to 100

Win Probability Added (WPAs): basically like WRC but weighted for the importance of each at bat in terms of how it changed its team's win probability

Clutch hitting ability (clutchs): Measures how much better or worse a player's performance in high stress environments is than his overall performance

Pitcher Stats

Wins above replacement by a pitcher (WARs_pitcher): derived using FIP adjusted for park and innings pitched to create an estimate of how many runs better the pitcher has been than a replacement level minor leaguer

Earned runs allowed per 9 innings (ERAs): derived by taking the number of earned runs allowed, normalizing to per game by multiplying by 9, and then dividing by number of innings pitched

Innings Pitched (IPs): the number of innings a pitcher has thrown in his career

Games Started (GSs): the number of games a pitcher has started in his career

Fielding Independent Pitching (FIPs): estimates a pitcher's abilities normalized for the defense playing behind him Takes into account home runs, walks, strikeouts, and hit by pitch Formula: ((13*HR)+(3*(BB+HBP))-(2*K))/IP + constant The constant normalizes FIP to ERA so the two can be compared and is usually around 3.10

ERA to FIP spread (E_Fs): a way to measure how good or bad the team behind a pitcher was at fielding

Strikeouts per 9 innings percentage (KK_Pers): a way to measure team-independent pitching ability

Walks per 9 innings rate (BB_Pers): a way to measure control and overall pitching ability
